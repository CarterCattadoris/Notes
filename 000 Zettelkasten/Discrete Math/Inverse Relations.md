Let $R \subseteq X \times Y$ be a relation
The inverse of R is the relation $R^{-1} \subseteq Y \times X$ defined by:
$$
R^{-1} = \{ (y,x):(x,y)\in R \}
$$
## Example:
Consider the following relations: 

T = {(a, 1), (b, 4), (d, 4), (d, 8)}  
S = {(4, 3), (8, 5), (7, 4), (8, 2)
  
$S^{−1} = \{(1, a), (4, b), (4, d), (8, d)\}$
$S^{−1} = \{(3, 4), (5, 8), (4, 7), (2, 8)\}$ 
$∅^{−1} = ∅$
