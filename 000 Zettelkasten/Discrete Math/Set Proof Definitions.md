Given sets A,B and arbitrary element w
- $w \in A \cup B$
	- $w \in A$ or $w \in B$
- $w \in A \cap B$
	- $w \in A$ and $w \in B$
- $w \in A - B$
	- If $w \in A$, then $w \not\in B$ 
- $w \in A \triangle B$
	- 
- $w \in A \times B$
	- There are (specific) elements $a\in A$ and $b\in B$ such that $w=(a,b)$ 
- $w \in 2^A$ 
	- $w \subseteq A$
